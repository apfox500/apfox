I still remember the first computer I took apart: It had all sorts of cool-looking green boards and wires that I had no clue what they did. I put it back together, and that computer never worked again. Now I'm a lot better at the reassembly part, but I still like to take things apart; especially computers.

One of my biggest interests is understanding how and why something works, I'm the type of guy to go down a Wikipedia rabbit-hole just to learn about the process of making snow, or what that smell after a rainstorm is (it's petrichor).

Now I'm a student in Computer Science and Electrical & Computer Engineering at CU Boulder. And I'm still taking apart computers, but I'm building them too. Working on both the hardware and software of computers I get to see how everything meshes together to create one, unified system. I like to look at a problem and break it down, then find not just a solution but the best one I can.

To my friends I'm best known as the guy who can probably help you with your homework, no matter the class. I'm also known as the adventurous one, because I'm always trying to ski a harder couloir, or climb a bigger face. But to me I'm just happiest when I'm pushing myself, whether that be physical or mental limits.

I channel these desires into my work through a simple approach: continually seeking innovation and learning opportunities. I constantly strive to create something new and to derive valuable lessons from every endeavor.
